# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Self Apply Center primarily serves Nepali students who are planning international education and need clear guidance before choosing a destination, course, university, or application pathway. Many visitors are at an early decision stage: comparing USA, Canada, UK, and South Korea, checking whether their profile is realistic, and looking for trustworthy help without losing control of the process.

Parents and guardians are a secondary audience. They need confidence that the process is transparent, financially understandable, and supported by people who can explain the reasoning behind each recommendation.

## Product Purpose

The website exists to present Self Apply Center as a professional education consultancy and guided self-application platform. It should help students understand SAC's core services, compare priority destinations, see proof through testimonials, ask questions, and begin an application through the SAC portal.

Success means visitors quickly understand what SAC does, trust that the process is clear and student-centered, and take a next step: start an application, send a WhatsApp message, submit an enquiry, or visit the office.

## Positioning

SAC helps students self-apply with expert guidance, transparent decision-making, and practical support from profile review to visa readiness.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: Primary is **Start Application** through the SAC application portal. Secondary is **WhatsApp / Contact SAC** for students who need counselling before starting.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: Your study-abroad application should feel clear, guided, and still in your control.
- Belief ladder: SAC understands my education goal; SAC focuses on the right countries and courses for my profile; SAC can review documents and explain next steps clearly; SAC has helped students before; starting with SAC is low-friction and practical.
- Proof on hand: Existing testimonials from previous SAC website content, including students with USA and Canada visa approvals; SAC contact details and office location at Star Mall, Putalisadak, Kathmandu; application portal at `https://sac.osom.global/1/student`.

## Brand Personality

The brand should feel clear, trustworthy, and empowering. The voice is direct, calm, and helpful, with enough confidence to guide students through serious decisions without sounding pushy or generic.

Emotionally, the site should reduce confusion. A student should feel that their choices can be organized into a manageable roadmap and that SAC will explain the process instead of hiding it.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic education-consultancy templates, stock-heavy pages that feel interchangeable, excessive animation, vague success claims, and anything that looks like a quick AI-generated or "vibecoding" website. Avoid being too flashy, too corporate-cold, or too busy for students and parents to scan.

## Design Principles

- Make the path visible: every major page should help students understand what happens next.
- Explain recommendations, not just services: SAC's value is clarity and reasoning, not only application submission.
- Lead with the core destinations: USA, Canada, UK, and South Korea should feel like the main pathways.
- Build trust through specifics: contact details, office location, testimonials, process stages, and realistic guidance matter more than inflated promises.
- Keep control with the student: the interface should support self-application confidence while showing expert help is available.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The website should use readable contrast, clear form labels, keyboard-accessible navigation, reduced-motion support, responsive layouts, and plain language. Motion should enhance understanding without blocking content for users who prefer less animation.
